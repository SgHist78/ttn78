The House of Tan Tang Niah

Draft : version 1.6
Date: 16 Mar 2026

Tan Tang Niah (TTN) was a prominent Chinese community leader in early 20th‑century Singapore. He was also a JP, a confectionery manufacturer, and owned a rubber plantation in Johore. The family residence was 40 Kerbau Road, (later renumberted to 37 Kerbau Rd). He died in 1925 at the age of 63 (Fig 02). Additional details :

  • President of Chung Cheng School
  • assisted in Cheong Poon Girls School
  • Hon Treasurer of Chinese High School
  • an active member of the Chinese Chamber of Commerce
  • President of Poh Chek Kiong association
  • member of Poh Leong association
  • a Justice of the Peace (JP)

.

.

.

TTN married Mdm Teo Hong Beng , to whom he bequeathed the family home at 37 Kerbau Rd. This home was later designated a National Monument and later acquired by the Singapore Government.

.

Mdm Teo Hong Beng passed away 30 Jun 1940.

Tan Sim Eng (S. E. Tan)

Tan Sim Eng (S. E. Tan)

.

.

Second son of TTN. Queen’s Scholar (1933), University of Cambridge. Became a Lawyer, Secretary of Chinese Bankers Trust (1938), and volunteer Machine Gunner with the Straits Settlements Volunteer Force (SSVF).

.

He was detained during the Japanese Sook Ching operation after the fall of Singapore in 1942 and never seen again. His name appears at the Kranji War Memorial.

.

Note: Tens of thousands of male Chinese of fighting age were murdered by the Japanese Secret Police shortly after the fall of Singapore on 14th Feb 1942. This massacre is known as the Sook Ching massacre.

    Tan Peng Choo (P. C. Tan)

    Tan P. C. (P. C. Tan)

    P C Tan was the 4th son of TTN. As a youth, he assisted his father with money‑collection errands. He gained familiarity with many prominent merchants of the time.

    .

    .

    .

    Pre war Years

    Singapore was bombed by the Japanese in late 1941 during the Battle for Singapore. 40 Kerbau Road (later renumbered 37 Kerbau Road) served as an ARP (Air Raid Precaution) Post during this time. P. C. was appointed the ARP Warden for a Kandang Kerbau sub zone (Fig 13 above).

    .

    As ARP Warden, part of P C's duty was to check at night for naked lights from houses. Recalcitrant Households who flouted the prevailing wartime "Blackout" regulation were charged in Court. Naked lights in time of war put the entire community at risk.
    .
    People in the neighborhood who were wounded, homeless or needed help also went to see P C. He told stories of driving his car to send the wounded to hospital, or to the docks to pick up supplies, at times, even when bombs were falling.

    .

    After the fall of Singapore, He managed to evade the Sook Ching dragnet, and escaped to the Cameron Highlands.

    .

    Postwar Years

    He returned to Singapore in 1946, after the British recaptured Singapore. He started a successful business, and little by little, rebuilt the family house, which had been looted, and in a bad state.

    He later took on teaching both full-time and part‑time when business became poor. He passed away in the mid-1970s

    .

    Education

    • Studied in ACS, passed Senior Cambridge, 1929 (Fig 15)
    • Passed Raffles College, 1932 (Fig 16)

    Pre-War Working Life

    • Helped run his father's rubber plantation in Johore
    • Taught Basic English (Fig 17)

    • Active contributor to local community (Fig 18)
    • Keen chess player (Fig 19)

    .

    Wartime Service

    • ARP Warden, Kandang Kerbau Zone, during Battle for Singapore, 1941/42 (Fig 14)

    Post War

    • He ran a successful business, restored Kerbau Road, bit by bit
    • In the early 1950s, Singapore was not as safe and secure as it is today. PC told of times when gangsters openly came to his office in broad daylight, and demanded "protection money" (Fig 20).
    • In the late 1950s, P C and his young family moved out of Kerbau Road and went to stay in the Katong/Joo Chiat area
    • In the early 1970s, in semi-retirement, P C also took time to enjoy road trips.
    • He passed on in the mid 1970s.

    Kerbau Road and nearby Places Today

    Kerbau Road and nearby Places Today

    • Today, this unique house is a popular spot for tourists
      .
    • There are many boutique hotels a short walk away
      .
    • It is a short 5 minute walk to the MRT (Little India Station)
      .
    • Another minute's walk brings you to the popular Tekka Market and Food Centre
      .
    • The nearby Farrer Park, adjacent to Race Course Road, had, in 1919, been Singapore's Race Course.
      .
      Aviation History was made here, when, on 4 Dec 1919, at 4:48 PM, a Vickers plane, piloted by a highly decorated World War I RAF veteran, 26 year old Cpt Ross Smith, accompanied by a crew of 3, successfully landed onto this Race Course. The plane taxied to the Race Course Grandstand. There Cpt Ross and crew were greeted by Officials, and a large cheering crowd. The crowd had been gathering since early afternoon to watch this historic event.      .
      .
      Cpt Ross and crew were taking part in the London to Darwin Air Race Competition, organised by the Australian Govt.
      .
      A few days later, Cpt Ross and his crew took off for Darwin. They made history by winning the London to Darwin Air Race Competition.
      .
    • This historic Air Race highlighted Singapore's natural strategic geographic location.. (It is roughly half the distance between Calcutta and Darwin).
      .
    • The area next to today's Farrer Park had once been dubbed as "Little England". This area was bounded by Gloucester Road, Dorset Road, Norfolk Road and Owen Road.
      .
      It included many roads named after British places, eg, Cambridge Road, Oxford Road, Kent Road, Hertford Road, Bristol Road, and Hampshire Road.
      .
      There used to be a Northumberland Road, leading to an old Hawker Centre. Both have now made way for new HDB estates.
      .
    • The Pek Kio Hawker Centre, at junction of Cambridge Road and Owen Road, is a very popular place. You will see a lot of taxis parked in the area during lunchtime.
      .
      The Hokien mee stall there has a Michelin star. Although the stall has many customers, their service is fast.
      .
